Research profile

Luca Busino, PhD

Associate Professor of Cancer Biology · Principal Investigator
Email coming soon

Luca Busino has studied the ubiquitin pathway and protein degradation for more than two decades. His laboratory investigates how E3 ubiquitin ligases control cell proliferation, cell fate, and malignant transformation, with a particular focus on hematologic cancers.

His research program connects basic mechanisms of ubiquitin signaling with drug discovery. The lab aims to understand how cancer cells become dependent on altered degradation pathways and how those dependencies can be converted into smarter therapeutic strategies, including molecular glues, PROTACs, and biologic degraders for precision medicine.

Fun fact: when he is not in the lab or with his family, he is probably on the soccer pitch.
