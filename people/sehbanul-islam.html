Sehbanul Islam, PhD

Postdoctoral Researcher · Ubiquitin Signaling and Precision Oncology
Email coming soon

Sehbanul Islam is a postdoctoral researcher in cancer biology with a background in cell and molecular biology. His research investigates how ubiquitin-dependent pathways regulate cancer cell proliferation, genome stability, and therapeutic response.

In the Busino Lab, Sehbanul combines functional genetics, biochemical assays, proteomics, and degradation-based pharmacology to identify cancer-specific vulnerabilities. His work is focused on understanding how E3 ligases and induced protein degradation can be exploited therapeutically in hematologic malignancies and other cancer contexts.

His projects emphasize both mechanism and translation: defining how degradation pathways are rewired in cancer cells, determining which nodes are druggable, and testing whether targeted protein degradation can produce deeper or more selective therapeutic responses than conventional inhibition.

Fun fact: when he is away from the bench, he is usually drawn to destinations with a beach.
