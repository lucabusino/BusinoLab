Thilan Trinh, PhD

Postdoctoral Researcher · Biologic Degraders and Cell-Surface Protein Degradation
Thilan.Trinh@PennMedicine.upenn.edu

Thilan Trinh earned her PhD in Molecular Biology after completing her undergraduate training in Biotechnology. Her research focuses on developing biologic degraders that target disease-associated cell-surface proteins for cancer therapy.

She develops protein-based therapeutics by integrating computational design with experimental validation. Her work connects binder engineering, receptor engagement, cellular trafficking, and lysosomal biology to define how extracellular and membrane proteins can be selectively eliminated from cancer or immune cells.

In the Busino Lab, Lan investigates receptor trafficking and lysosomal degradation pathways to enable targeted protein degradation beyond the intracellular proteasome. Her goal is to establish new biologic degrader strategies for hematologic malignancies, with emphasis on therapeutic selectivity, mechanism of action, and translational potential.

Fun fact: when she is not in the lab, she is probably learning Korean or exploring new restaurants in search of great food.
