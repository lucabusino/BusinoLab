Jiadong Tao, PhD

Lab Member · Membrane Protein Degradation and Cancer Immunotherapy
Email coming soon

Jiadong Tao earned his PhD in Molecular Immunology from China Agricultural University and joined the Busino Lab in 2022. His research focuses on developing degraders that eliminate membrane and extracellular proteins relevant to cancer immunotherapy.

He engineers strategies that redirect disease-relevant surface receptors into endosomal trafficking and lysosomal degradation pathways. By removing immunoregulatory receptors rather than simply blocking them, his work tests whether lysosomal degradation can produce stronger, more durable, or mechanistically distinct effects on immune-cell function.

Jiadong combines molecular design, flow cytometry, cell-based degradation assays, biochemical validation, and functional immune assays to define which membrane targets can be degraded, how degradation changes signaling, and whether this approach can improve anti-tumor immunity in lymphoma, myeloma, and CAR-T-related settings.

Fun fact: when he is not working in the lab, he might be cooking hot pot at home.
